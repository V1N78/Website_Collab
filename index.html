Nelson Lakes

Nelson Lakes National Park is a treasure trove of natural beauty, located in the northern part of New Zealand's South Island. It's renowned for its stunning alpine landscape, native forests, and serene lakes.

The Lakes

Rotoroa

Lake Rotoroa is a gem within the Nelson Lakes National Park. It's known for its tranquil beauty and is the larger of the two lakes in the park, the other being Lake Rotoiti. Compared to the more frequently visited Lake Rotoiti, Lake Rotoroa offers a serene and less crowded environment. It's perfect for those seeking solitude and a deep connection with nature. The lake is also popular for trout fishing, and you can also enjoy boating on its calm waters. The area around Lake Rotoroa is rich in native birdlife. You might spot species like the New Zealand robin, tui, and bellbird. The forested areas also provide habitat for other native animals and plants. There are remnants of early European settlement and Maori heritage sites around the lake. These offer a glimpse into the history and cultural significance of the area.

Rotoiti

Lake Rotoiti is a stunning destination within Nelson Lakes National Park. It's known for its breathtaking alpine scenery, crystal-clear waters, and a wide range of outdoor activities. Lake Rotoiti is a popular spot for boating, fishing, swimming, and kayaking. The nearby Mount Robert offers fantastic hiking trails, including the Mount Robert Circuit, which provides panoramic views of the lake and surrounding mountains. The small village of St Arnaud is located near the northern end of Lake Rotoiti. It serves as the main gateway to the park and offers accommodation, amenities, and visitor information. The St Arnaud Range provides a picturesque backdrop to the village and the lake. The iconic Kerr Bay jetty at Lake Rotoiti is a favorite spot for photographs and a starting point for several walking tracks. The Bellbird Walk and Honeydew Walk are short, easy trails that explore the beech forest and the unique honeydew ecosystem. The area around Lake Rotoiti is rich in native flora and fauna. You can spot birds such as the New Zealand robin, tui, and bellbird. The lake itself is home to both brown and rainbow trout, making it a popular fishing spot. There are several campsites around the lake, offering a chance to immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the area. These campsites are well-equipped with basic facilities and provide a great base for exploring the park. Lake Rotoiti's tranquil setting and diverse recreational opportunities make it a must-visit destination in New Zealand's South Island.

The Trails

Robert Ridge

The Robert Ridge Trail in Nelson Lakes National Park is a challenging and rewarding route for experienced adventurers. This trail takes you from Mount Robert Roadend to Angelus Hut, situated on the shores of Lake Angelus, 1650 meters above sea level. The trail is known for its steep, rocky, and exposed sections, making it suitable for well-equipped trampers only. The best times to visit are from December through April, as the trail can be hazardous with snow and ice during other times of the year. Avalanches are most common in winter and spring but can occur in any season, so it's recommended to contact the Rotoiti / Nelson Lakes Visitor Centre for advice on conditions before you go. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding alpine environment and is popular for backpacking, camping, and hiking.

Cascade Route

The Cascade Route, also known as the Cascade Track or Hukere Stream, is a challenging and rewarding hike in Nelson Lakes National Park. This expert-level trail takes you from Lakehead Hut or Coldwater Hut up to Lake Angelus. Along the way, you'll encounter breathtaking waterfalls, crystal-clear water, and stunning views. From the Cascade Track junction, it's a steady 4-hour climb following the Hukere Stream. When you reach the bush line, the track ends, but you can follow the steep route to Angelus Hut marked by poles. This hike is not for the faint-hearted, but the rewards are well worth the effort. If you're up for the challenge, the Cascade Route offers an unforgettable adventure in one of New Zealand's most beautiful national parks.

Where to Stay

St Arnaud

St. Arnaud is a picturesque village located in the Nelson Lakes National Park in the South Island of New Zealand. Nestled beside Lake Rotoiti, it serves as a gateway to some of the most stunning alpine scenery and outdoor adventures in the region. The area is renowned for its pristine lakes, native forests, and towering mountains, making it a haven for hikers, birdwatchers, and nature enthusiasts. With its charming atmosphere and access to a range of recreational activities, St. Arnaud is a perfect destination for those seeking tranquility and natural beauty.

Angelus Hut

Angelus Hut is a popular destination located in the Nelson Lakes National Park in the Nelson/Tasman region of New Zealand. The hut is situated at an elevation of 1,650 meters, on the shores of Lake Angelus, surrounded by spectacular mountain views. There are several routes to reach Angelus Hut, with the most popular being the Pinchgut Track and Robert Ridge Route. These routes offer stunning views but can be challenging, especially in winter when snow and ice are common. The area around Angelus Hut is perfect for hiking, tramping, and enjoying the natural beauty of the Nelson Lakes National Park
